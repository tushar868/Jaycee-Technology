Radar Level Transmitter

Download Literature

Radar level transmitters are widely used in various industries to measure the level of liquids, solids, and slurries in tanks, vessels, and other containers. They provide accurate and reliable level measurements, making them a popular choice for process control and inventory management. In this guide, we will delve into the working principles, types, applications, and benefits of radar level transmitters.

Working Principle

Radar level transmitters operate on the principle of time-of-flight (TOF) technology. They emit high-frequency electromagnetic waves, typically in the microwave range, and measure the time it takes for the waves to travel to the target and return. By knowing the speed of electromagnetic waves in the medium, the instrument can accurately calculate the distance and, consequently, the level of the substance being measured.

  • Beam angle : 8 Deg

  • Ingress Protection : IP65

Application

Oil and Gas : They are used for level measurement in storage tanks, oil refineries, and offshore platforms. They can handle challenging environments and measure levels accurately in both crude oil and refined products.

Chemical Industry : Radar transmitters are employed for measuring levels of various chemicals, acids, solvents, and corrosive substances in storage tanks and vessels.

Water and Wastewater : They are utilized in water treatment plants, reservoirs, and wastewater treatment facilities to monitor water levels, ensuring efficient operation and preventing overflow or shortages.

Food and Beverage : Radar transmitters are employed in the food industry for measuring levels of liquids, such as milk, beer, wine, and sauces, as well as solids like grains, powders, and granules.

Pharmaceuticals : Radar level transmitters are used for level control in pharmaceutical manufacturing processes, including the measurement of liquid ingredients, bulk solids, and intermediate products.

Features

Accuracy : Radar transmitters provide highly accurate level measurements, even in challenging conditions, such as turbulence, foam, or vapors. They are not affected by factors like temperature, pressure, or composition changes.

Non-Contact Measurement : Non-contact radar transmitters eliminate the need for physical contact with the substance being measured. This feature makes them suitable for applications involving corrosive or abrasive materials or those requiring hygienic conditions.

Versatility : Radar level transmitters can be used in a wide range of industries and applications, accommodating various process conditions, materials, and vessel geometries.

Reliability : These transmitters offer reliable and continuous level measurements, reducing the risk of spills, overflows, and process disruptions. They have long service lives and require minimal maintenance.

Easy Installation : Radar level transmitters are relatively easy to install and set up. They can be mounted on the top of the tank or vessel, eliminating the need for invasive installation procedures.
