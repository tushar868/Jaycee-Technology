Capacitance Level Transmitter

Download Literature

Capacitance level transmitters are widely used in industries that require accurate and reliable measurements of liquid levels in tanks, vessels, and pipelines. These transmitters utilize the principle of capacitance to provide continuous level monitoring and control.

Working Principle

Capacitance level transmitters operate based on the principle of capacitance, which is the ability of a system to store an electrical charge. These transmitters consist of a probe or sensing element and a reference electrode. The probe is inserted into the tank or vessel, and the reference electrode is placed in close proximity. The space between the probe and the reference electrode forms a capacitor. When the tank is empty, the capacitance is low. As the liquid level rises and covers the probe, the capacitance increases proportionally to the level of the liquid. This change in capacitance is then converted into an electrical signal, which is used to determine and indicate the level of the liquid.

Application

Oil and Gas : They are used in oil refineries, petrochemical plants, and storage terminals to measure levels of crude oil, refined products, liquefied gases, and chemicals in tanks and pipelines.

Water and Wastewater : Capacitance level transmitters are employed in water treatment plants, wastewater treatment facilities, and reservoirs to monitor levels of water, chemicals, sludge, and other liquids, ensuring efficient water management and preventing overflow or shortage.

Food and Beverage : They find applications in the food and beverage industry for measuring levels of liquids such as milk, juices, sauces, oils, as well as bulk solids like grains, powders, and granules.

Pharmaceuticals : Capacitance level transmitters are utilized in pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities to measure levels of liquid ingredients, intermediates, and final products in tanks and vessels, ensuring accurate formulation and production control.

Chemical Processing : They are employed in chemical plants to monitor levels of various chemicals, solvents, acids, and corrosive liquids in storage tanks, reactors, and process vessels.

Features

Probe Variations : Capacitance level transmitters offer various probe designs, including rigid rods, flexible cables, and coaxial cables. This allows for flexibility in selecting the most suitable probe type for the specific application and tank geometry.

Multiple Mounting Options : These transmitters can be mounted from the top, bottom, or side of the tank, providing versatility in installation and ensuring compatibility with different tank configurations.

Temperature and Pressure Resistance : Capacitance level transmitters are designed to withstand a wide range of temperatures and pressures, making them suitable for diverse industrial environments.

Multiple Output Options : They provide various output options, such as analog signals (4-20mA, 0-10V) or digital signals (Modbus, HART), facilitating seamless integration with control systems and data acquisition devices.

Calibration and Configuration : Capacitance level transmitters often feature calibration and configuration capabilities, allowing for easy setup, adjustment, and calibration to meet specific application requirements.
