Vibrating Fork Level Limit Switch For Food Grains

Download Literature

In the food industry, maintaining precise control over material levels is crucial for efficient production, preventing spills, and ensuring product quality. When it comes to detecting the level of food grains, a vibrating fork level limit switch proves to be an excellent choice. This innovative device offers reliable and accurate level detection specifically tailored for food grain applications.

How does a vibrating fork level limit switch work?

A vibrating fork level limit switch consists of a fork-shaped probe made of robust and food-grade materials, such as stainless steel. The fork probe is designed to vibrate at its natural frequency when no grains are present. The switch is typically installed in a silo, bin, or hopper in such a way that the fork probe extends into the food grain.

When the food grains cover or surround the fork probe, it dampens its vibrations, altering the natural frequency. This change in vibration frequency is detected by an electronic circuit, which triggers an output signal indicating the presence or absence of the food grains at the desired level.
