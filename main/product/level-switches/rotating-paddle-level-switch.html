Rotating Paddle Level Switch

Download Literature

A rotating paddle level switch is an essential device used for monitoring and controlling the level of liquids in tanks, vessels, and pipelines. It operates based on the principle of a rotating paddle that senses the presence or absence of liquid at a specific level. This reliable and versatile switch is widely utilized in various industries where precise liquid level detection is crucial.

Working Principle

The rotating paddle level switch consists of a motor-driven paddle assembly and a switch mechanism. The paddle assembly typically comprises a motor, a shaft, and a rotating paddle blade. The switch mechanism is designed to respond to the movement or lack thereof in the paddle assembly.

When the switch is powered on, the motor rotates the paddle at a predetermined speed. As the liquid level changes, the rotating paddle comes into contact with the liquid surface. The resistance of the liquid causes the paddle to stop rotating or slow down, which is detected by the switch mechanism. This change in paddle movement triggers the switch to indicate the presence or absence of liquid at the desired level.

Application

Industrial Processes These switches are extensively used in industries such as chemical processing, water treatment, oil and gas, and food and beverage. They enable precise control of liquid levels, preventing overfilling or underfilling of tanks, ensuring efficient production processes, and protecting equipment from damage.

Pump Control : Rotating paddle level switches play a crucial role in pump control systems. They monitor the liquid level in pump sumps or reservoirs, preventing dry running or overflow situations. The switch can trigger the pump to start or stop based on the liquid level, ensuring optimal pump performance and preventing costly failures.

Storage Tanks : They are commonly employed in storage tanks for inventory management and overflow prevention. These switches provide reliable level indication and can activate alarms or control valves to maintain the desired liquid level, preventing spills or tank overfilling.

Water and Wastewater Management : Rotating paddle level switches are utilized in water and wastewater treatment facilities to monitor liquid levels in various tanks, basins, and reservoirs. They ensure proper flow control, prevent equipment damage, and support efficient water management processes.
